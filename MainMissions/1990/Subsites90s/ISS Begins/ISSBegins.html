ISS Begins

Launch: December 4, 1998

Mission Duration: 12 Days, 22 Hours, 20 Minutes

Landing: December 15, 1998

STS-88 aboard Space Shuttle Endeavour was the first assembly mission to the International Space Station. It delivered and mated the U.S. Unity module to the already-in-orbit Russian Zarya module, performed three spacewalks to connect power, data and thermal control umbilicals, and paved the way for all subsequent ISS assembly flights.
