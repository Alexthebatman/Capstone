NEW HORIZONS

Launch: January 19th, 2006

Mission Duration: Ongoing

Retired: N/A

New Horizons was the first spacecraft to explore Pluto up close, providing humanity's first detailed images and data from the dwarf planet in 2015. After its historic Pluto flyby, it continued deeper into the Kuiper Belt, flying by Arrokoth in 2019. The spacecraft revealed Pluto's icy mountains, vast plains, and hazy atmosphere, completely rewriting what we knew of the distant world. New Horizons remains active, sending data from the outer solar system and continuing to explore far beyond Neptune.
