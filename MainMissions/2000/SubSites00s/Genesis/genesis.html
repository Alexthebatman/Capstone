GENESIS

Launch: August 8th, 2001

Mission Duration: 3 Years, 1 Month, 6 Days

Retired: September 13th, 2004

The Genesis mission was NASA's first attempt to return samples from beyond the Moon. It launched in 2001 to collect solar wind particles using ultra-pure materials in space. After collecting solar wind ions for over two years, its return capsule crashed in Utah due to parachute failure, damaging some samples. However, scientists were still able to recover valuable data about solar composition, offering new insights into the birth of our solar system.
