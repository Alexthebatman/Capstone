OPPORTUNITY ROVER

Launch: July 7th, 2003

Mission Duration: 14 Years, 7 Months, 19 Days

Retired: February 13th, 2019

Opportunity, or MER-B, was NASA's twin to Spirit, launched just weeks later and landed in Meridiani Planum on January 25, 2004. Like Spirit, it was designed for a 90-sol mission but blew past expectations, operating for over 14 years. Opportunity made groundbreaking discoveries including signs of ancient water, hematite-rich spherules ("blueberries"), and sedimentary rocks. The mission officially ended after a massive Martian dust storm in 2018 caused it to lose power and contact.
