STEREO

Launch: October 25th, 2006

Mission Duration: 19 Years+ (Ongoing)

Retired: N/A

STEREO (Solar TErrestrial RElations Observatory) launched two nearly identical spacecraft in 2006 to orbit the Sun, one ahead of Earth and one behind. This twin setup allowed for the first-ever 3D imaging of solar storms and coronal mass ejections. The mission advanced space weather forecasting and gave scientists new insights into solar dynamics. STEREO-B lost contact in 2014, but STEREO-A remains operational and continues to deliver valuable data.
