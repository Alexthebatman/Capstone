MARS RECONNAISSANCE ORBITER

Launch: August 12th, 2005

Mission Duration: Ongoing

Retired: N/A

The Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter (MRO) was launched by NASA to study Mars' atmosphere, surface, and subsurface with unparalleled detail. Its powerful HiRISE camera captured images of Martian terrain at resolutions high enough to observe individual rocks. MRO has been vital for scouting future landing sites, tracking weather, and relaying communications for rovers like Curiosity and Perseverance. It continues to operate today, serving as one of the most productive missions in Mars exploration history.
