SPIRIT ROVER

Launch: June 10th, 2003

Mission Duration: 6 Years, 2 Months, 19 Days

Retired: March 22nd, 2010

Spirit, also known as MER-A, was one of two rovers in NASA's Mars Exploration Rover program. It launched on June 10, 2003, and successfully landed on January 4, 2004, in Gusev Crater. Designed for a 90-day mission, Spirit operated for over six years, far exceeding expectations. It analyzed Martian rocks, soil, and geology, providing crucial evidence of past water activity. The rover got stuck in soft soil in 2009 and eventually lost communication in 2010.
