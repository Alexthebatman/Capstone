APOLLO 14

Launch: January 31st, 1971

Mission Duration: 9 Days, 1 Hour, 58 Minutes

Retired: February 9th, 1971

Apollo 14 marked NASA’s third successful Moon landing, carrying astronauts Alan Shepard, Edgar Mitchell, and Stuart Roosa. Shepard famously hit two golf balls on the lunar surface. The crew conducted extensive scientific experiments and collected lunar samples from the Fra Mauro formation, originally intended for Apollo 13.
