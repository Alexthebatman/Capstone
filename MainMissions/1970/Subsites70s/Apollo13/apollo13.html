APOLLO 13

Launch: April 11th, 1970

Mission Duration: 5 Days, 22 Hours, 54 Minutes

Retired: April 17th, 1970

Apollo 13 was the seventh crewed mission in NASA's Apollo program and was intended to be the third to land on the Moon. Launched on April 11, 1970, the mission was aborted after an oxygen tank in the service module exploded. Despite the life-threatening failure, the crew—Jim Lovell, Jack Swigert, and Fred Haise—returned safely after a harrowing journey, making Apollo 13 a "successful failure" that showcased NASA's ingenuity under pressure.
