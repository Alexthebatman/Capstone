VOYAGER 1 & 2

Launch: Voyager 2: August 20, 1977 | Voyager 1: September 5, 1977

Mission Duration: Ongoing (both active as of 2025)

Retired: N/A

The Voyager program launched two robotic probes to explore the outer planets and beyond. Voyager 2 visited Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune, becoming the only spacecraft to do so. Voyager 1, now the farthest human-made object from Earth, provided detailed images of Jupiter and Saturn before continuing on its interstellar mission. Both carry Golden Records—time capsules of Earth’s culture—intended for any intelligent extraterrestrial life that might find them.
