APOLLO 16

Launch: April 16th, 1972

Mission Duration: 11 Days, 1 Hour, 51 Minutes

Retired: April 27th, 1972

Apollo 16 was NASA’s fifth mission to land humans on the Moon, targeting the Descartes Highlands. Astronauts John Young and Charles Duke conducted three EVAs using the Lunar Roving Vehicle, while Ken Mattingly remained in orbit. The mission gathered over 200 pounds of lunar samples and helped scientists understand the Moon’s geological history beyond flat mare regions.
