APOLLO 15

Launch: July 26th, 1971

Mission Duration: 12 Days, 7 Hours, 11 Minutes

Retired: August 7th, 1971

Apollo 15 was the first of the “J missions,” featuring longer stays on the Moon and more advanced science. Astronauts David Scott and James Irwin used the Lunar Roving Vehicle for the first time, covering over 17 miles on the lunar surface while collecting valuable geological samples from the Hadley-Apennine region. Alfred Worden remained in lunar orbit aboard the command module.
