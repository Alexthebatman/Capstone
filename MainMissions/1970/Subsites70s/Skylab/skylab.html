SKYLAB

Launch: May 14th, 1973

Mission Duration: 6 Years in Orbit

Retired: July 11th, 1979

Skylab was the United Statesâ€™ first space station, launched on May 14, 1973. Across three crewed missions, astronauts conducted groundbreaking research in solar physics, Earth observation, and biomedical studies. Despite initial damage during launch, Skylab proved a success and laid the groundwork for future orbital habitats like the International Space Station.
