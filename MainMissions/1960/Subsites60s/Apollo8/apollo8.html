APOLLO 8

Launch: December 21, 1968

Mission Duration: 6 days, 3 hours, 0 minutes & 42 seconds

Retired: December 27, 1968

Apollo 8 was the first crewed spacecraft to leave Earth orbit, reach the Moon, orbit it, and return safely to Earth. Commanded by Frank Borman with crew members James Lovell and William Anders, the mission paved the way for Apollo 11 by testing crucial systems in lunar orbit. It’s most famous for capturing the iconic “Earthrise” photo, symbolizing humanity’s perspective shift on our planet.
