APOLLO 1

Launch: N/A (Planned for February 21st, 1967)

Mission Duration: 0 Days (Ground Test Failure)

Retired: January 27th, 1967

Apollo 1 was intended to be the first crewed mission of NASA's Apollo program. Tragically, on January 27, 1967, a cabin fire during a launch rehearsal test claimed the lives of astronauts Gus Grissom, Ed White, and Roger B. Chaffee. The disaster led to major design overhauls and safety improvements across NASA's entire spaceflight program.
