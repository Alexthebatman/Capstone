FREEDOM 7 (MERCURY-REDSTONE 3)

Launch: May 5th, 1961

Mission Duration: 15 Minutes & 22 Seconds

Retired: May 15th, 1963

On May 5, 1961, NASA's Mercury-Redstone 3 mission, known as Freedom 7, launched Alan Shepard into a suborbital flight, making him the first American in space. This 15-minute mission demonstrated the United States' entry into manned spaceflight and laid the groundwork for future orbital missions.
