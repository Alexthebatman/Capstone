MMU SPACEWALKS (STS-41B)

Launch: February 3rd, 1984

Mission Duration: 7 Days, 23 Hours, 15 Minutes

Retired: February 11th, 1984

STS-41B, flown aboard the Space Shuttle Challenger, featured the first untethered spacewalk using the Manned Maneuvering Unit (MMU). Astronaut Bruce McCandless II famously floated freely in space, becoming an iconic image of human spaceflight. This mission demonstrated new capabilities in EVA technology and satellite deployment procedures.
