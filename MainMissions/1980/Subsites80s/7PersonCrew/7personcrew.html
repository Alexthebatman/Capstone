7 PERSON CREW (STS-41G)

Launch: October 5th, 1984

Mission Duration: 8 Days, 5 Hours, 23 Minutes

Retired: October 13th, 1984

STS-41G was the first space shuttle mission to fly with a seven-member crew, including Kathryn Sullivan—the first American woman to perform a spacewalk. The crew aboard Challenger deployed the ERBS satellite and conducted Earth observation experiments. This mission demonstrated the shuttle program's capacity to support larger and more diverse teams in orbit, expanding the possibilities for scientific and operational collaboration.
