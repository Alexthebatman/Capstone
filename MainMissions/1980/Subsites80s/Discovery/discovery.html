DISCOVERY (STS-26)

Launch: September 29th, 1988

Mission Duration: 4 Days, 1 Hour, 0 Minutes

Retired: October 3rd, 1988

STS-26 marked NASA's "Return to Flight" following the Challenger disaster. Flown by Space Shuttle Discovery, the mission successfully deployed the TDRS-C communications satellite and validated numerous new safety protocols. The mission's success restored national confidence in the Space Shuttle program and marked a turning point in shuttle safety culture moving forward into the 1990s.
