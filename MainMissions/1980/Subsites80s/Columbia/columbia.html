COLUMBIA (STS-1)

Launch: April 12th, 1981

Mission Duration: 2 Days, 6 Hours, 20 Minutes

Retired: April 14th, 1981

STS-1 was the first orbital flight of NASA's Space Shuttle program, launching Columbia into space on April 12, 1981. Commanded by John Young and piloted by Robert Crippen, the mission marked the first time a crewed spacecraft was launched into orbit and returned for reuse. Despite encountering some heat shield damage on reentry, the mission was successful and paved the way for three decades of shuttle operations.
