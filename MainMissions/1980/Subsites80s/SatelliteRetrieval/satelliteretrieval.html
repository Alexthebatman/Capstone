SATELLITE RETRIEVAL (STS-51A)

Launch: November 8th, 1984

Mission Duration: 7 Days, 23 Hours, 44 Minutes

Retired: November 16th, 1984

STS-51A was the first space shuttle mission to retrieve and return satellites from orbit. Using astronaut spacewalks and the shuttle's robotic arm, the crew recovered two malfunctioning communication satellites: Palapa B2 and Westar VI.
