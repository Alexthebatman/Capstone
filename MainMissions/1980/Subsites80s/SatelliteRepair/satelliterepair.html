SATELLITE REPAIR (STS-41C)

Launch: April 6th, 1984

Mission Duration: 6 Days, 23 Hours, 40 Minutes

Retired: April 13th, 1984

STS-41C was the first space shuttle mission to perform an in-orbit satellite repair. Astronauts captured the malfunctioning Solar Maximum Mission (Solar Max) satellite, brought it into the shuttle bay, and conducted extensive repairs before redeploying it. The mission demonstrated the feasibility of complex servicing operations in space, paving the way for future missions like Hubble maintenance.
