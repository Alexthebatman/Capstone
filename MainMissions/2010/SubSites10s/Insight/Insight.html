INSIGHT

Launch: May 5th, 2018

Mission Duration: 4 Years, 7 Months

Retired: December 21st, 2022

NASA's InSight lander was the first mission dedicated to studying the deep interior of Mars. Using a sensitive seismometer and heat probe, InSight recorded over 1,300 marsquakes, helping scientists build the first detailed maps of the Red Planet’s internal structure. It also measured Mars' core size and crust thickness. The mission ended when dust covered the lander’s solar panels, depleting its power.
