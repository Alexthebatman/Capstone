ORION EFT-1

Launch: December 5th, 2014

Mission Duration: 4 hours, 24 minutes

Retired: December 5th, 2014

Orion EFT-1 marked the first test flight of NASA's Orion spacecraft, designed for future crewed deep space missions. Launched atop a Delta IV Heavy rocket, the uncrewed capsule orbited Earth twice, reaching an altitude of 3,600 miles before reentering the atmosphere at nearly 20,000 mph. The mission successfully validated key systems like heat shielding, avionics, and parachute deployment—critical for Orion’s future lunar and Mars missions.
