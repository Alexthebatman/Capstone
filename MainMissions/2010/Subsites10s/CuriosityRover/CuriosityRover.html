CURIOSITY

Launch: November 26th, 2011

Mission Duration: Active

Retired: N/A

NASA's Curiosity rover, part of the Mars Science Laboratory mission, was launched in 2011 and landed in Gale Crater on August 6, 2012. Its goals include studying Martian climate and geology and assessing whether Mars ever had conditions suitable for life. Curiosity is equipped with a suite of advanced instruments, including a laser spectrometer, environmental sensors, and a drill. The mission has revolutionized our understanding of Mars and continues to return groundbreaking science over a decade later.
