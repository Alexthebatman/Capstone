JUNO

Launch: August 5th, 2011

Mission Duration: Ongoing (as of 2025)

Retired: N/A

NASA's Juno mission was launched to study Jupiter's atmosphere, magnetic field, auroras, and interior structure. Arriving in Jupiter orbit on July 4, 2016, Juno uses a polar orbit to gather data while minimizing radiation damage. The spacecraft has made groundbreaking discoveries about Jupiter's deep weather systems, polar cyclones, and core composition. Originally a primary mission, it has been extended to continue exploring not only Jupiter but also its moons like Ganymede and Europa.
